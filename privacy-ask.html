The short version

No accounts. No servers. No analytics. No exceptions.

ASK is a single-app experience that runs entirely offline. There is no sign-up, no cloud sync, no telemetry, and no third-party SDK collecting anything in the background. If a privacy policy could be one sentence, ours would be: we don't have your data because we never asked for it.

Offline by design

01Who we are

ASK is an independent iOS application built for couples, friends, colleagues, and teammates who want to ask each other better questions. It is published by Cheuk Wang ("we," "us," or "our"), an independent developer.

This privacy notice describes — in plain English — what data ASK handles, why we don't ask for any of it, and what your rights are if you want to verify any of this.

02What data we collect

"We do not collect, transmit, or store any personal data about you on any server we control."

The only data ASK uses is local app state stored on your device — things like which card deck you last opened or your in-app preferences. This information never leaves your phone. It is not synced, backed up to our servers, or shared with anyone.

When you delete ASK from your device, all of that local state is removed along with it.

What this means for Apple's "App Privacy" section

ASK is registered with Apple as "Data Not Collected" across every category — contact info, identifiers, usage data, diagnostics, and everything else.

03How we use your data

Because no personal data is collected, there is nothing to use, process, sell, or share. The app reads its card content from a bundle shipped inside the app itself, which is why ASK works without an internet connection.

04Third-party services

ASK does not integrate any third-party services, analytics platforms, advertising networks, or cloud back-ends. There are no SDKs from Google, Meta, Amplitude, Mixpanel, Sentry, or anyone else inside this app.

The only third party involved is Apple, which distributes the app through the App Store. That relationship is governed by Apple's Privacy Policy, not ours.

05Your rights

Under the GDPR, CCPA, and similar regulations worldwide, you have a set of rights over your personal data. Because we hold none of your personal data, most of those rights are satisfied by default:

Right to access
There is no record of you for us to hand over.
Right to erasure
Deleting the app erases everything ASK stored.
Right to portability
No data exists in our systems to export.
Right to object
No data is processed, so nothing to object to.

If you have any questions about how these rights apply to you, please email us — contact details are at the bottom of this page.

06Children's privacy

ASK does not knowingly collect data from anyone, including children. Because no data is collected from any user at all, there is no risk of unintended collection from younger users.

Some questions in the deck are written for adults. We recommend parents and guardians review the content before sharing the app with younger users.

07California rights (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act gives you specific rights. For ASK specifically:

  • We do not sell personal information. Ever, to anyone.
  • We do not share personal information for cross-context behavioural advertising.
  • We do not collect sensitive personal information.

If you'd like to confirm any of this directly, you can email us at cheuk.coding@gmail.com.
